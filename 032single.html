External Resources

November 14, 2017 / by Huan Zhang

1. Interview with multilingual friend Zhang, Huan, and Muling He. “Conversing with the rising genius polyglot.” 1 Nov. 2017.
This is the primary source that I am going to cite in my position paper. I interviewed one friend who speaks at least five languages and asked his opinion on how he thinks multilingual ability helps him to express. Although the instruction of this paper didn’t suggest interview as an external resource, I do think the interview with a living example of multilingual person around us is a way better resource to illustrate an argument compared to any other distant materials on the internet. It’s more comprehensive since I will interact with a living person, and the topic of interview can be broader since it’s not a fixed source.

2. Jean-Marc Dewaele, The emotional weight of I love you in multilinguals’ languages, In Journal of Pragmatics, Volume 40, Issue 10, 2008, Pages 1753-1780, ISSN 0378-2166
This is really an interesting article – we can see that from the title. Its format is academic journal so it’s credible enough to provide information. Although it addresses only a special case in my whole topic, that’s “I love you”, the article still relates to the idea on how the multilingual express themselves. Its conclusion, “In that sense, the emotion script of love resembles the multicompetent users themselves, i.e. it is unique and ever- evolving” Is also standing in my position and going to support my claim. This source was found from the university digital library.
